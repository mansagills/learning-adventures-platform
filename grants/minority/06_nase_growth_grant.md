## Section 1: About My Business

### What I Do

I am the founder of Learning Adventures — a free, gamified educational platform for K–5 students. I built it as a self-employed entrepreneur, without institutional backing, because I saw a genuine problem and had the skills to solve it.

The platform gives students a 2D pixel-art school campus to explore — navigating a customizable avatar between Math, Science, English, History, and Interdisciplinary buildings, playing curriculum-aligned adventure games, and earning XP, badges, and achievements. It is free for every family. It runs on aging hardware and low-bandwidth connections. It is COPPA-compliant by design — anonymous usernames, PIN login, no advertising, no data collection on children.

The platform includes:
- **41 verified educational games and interactive lessons** — all free, all standards-aligned
- **A fully operational 2D campus world** (Phaser.js + Next.js 14) with a functional Math Building
- **Parent and teacher dashboards** — progress tracking, goal-setting, weekly family digests
- **AI-powered content studio** (Google Gemini) — generates new standards-aligned games without proportional cost increase
- **Social features** — leaderboards, study groups, friend challenges

### How I Generate Revenue

Learning Adventures uses a freemium model:
- **Free forever:** Full educational content, child accounts, basic dashboards
- **Premium tier (~$5–8/month):** Advanced analytics, early content access, premium cosmetics
- **School/district licensing ($3–5/student/year):** Teacher tools, class analytics, curriculum documentation

I am currently in the pre-revenue scaling phase — the platform is complete, the product is ready, and I am actively pursuing the first school partnerships and premium subscribers. The NASE Growth Grant would fund the outreach activities that convert a completed product into a revenue-generating business.

### My Business Stage and Goals

**Where I am:** Platform fully built and operational; pre-revenue; ready to scale

**12-month goals:**
1. 3,000+ active users (students) from underserved communities
2. 10+ school/district partnerships generating licensing revenue
3. First $10,000 in revenue from premium subscriptions and school licensing
4. Content library expanded from 41 to 75+ games and lessons

**The bottleneck:** The product is complete. What I need are the resources to reach the families and schools who benefit from it — and the business infrastructure to convert that reach into sustainable revenue.

## Section 2: The Problem I'm Solving — Why This Business Exists

The educational technology market is failing the students who need it most.

Premium edtech platforms cost $10–20/month — out of reach for families near the poverty line. Free alternatives are either low-engagement drill apps or "free" in name only, funded by advertising targeting children. Under-resourced schools can't afford platform licenses. And most edtech was built for a narrow slice of the population — culturally, linguistically, and economically.

The result: the children from low-income households, communities of color, and under-resourced schools — the children most at risk of disengaging from STEM by fifth grade — are the ones with the least access to tools that could change that trajectory.

Learning Adventures was built to solve that. A free, safe, genuinely engaging platform that works on aging hardware, protects children's privacy, represents diverse communities in its content, and — most importantly — is actually fun enough that kids choose to come back.

I built it as a self-employed founder because I believed the product was worth building even before the market proved it. The NASE Growth Grant would give me the resources to prove the market right.

## Section 3: How the Grant Funds Would Be Used

NASE Growth Grants are designed to fund specific, concrete business growth activities. Here is exactly how the $4,000 would be deployed:

### Use 1: School District Outreach Package ($2,000)

The fastest path to sustainable revenue for Learning Adventures is school and district licensing ($3–5/student/year). A single district partnership with 500 students generates $1,500–$2,500 in annual recurring revenue — and opens the door to a network of teachers and parents who become organic platform advocates.

The $2,000 would fund:
- **Professional pitch deck** (design + print): A polished, data-driven school pitch deck tailored to district curriculum coordinators and principals — showing curriculum alignment, usage data, safety compliance, and implementation ease
- **Outreach coordinator stipend** (2 months, part-time): A contract education outreach specialist to identify Title I schools in 3–5 target states, initiate contact, and schedule demonstration sessions
- **Demo environment setup**: A dedicated classroom demo environment on the platform allowing principals and teachers to experience the platform as a student would — critical for conversion

**Expected outcome:** 5–8 school partnership conversations initiated; 2–3 partnerships closed within 90 days

### Use 2: Email Marketing Infrastructure ($1,000)

Email is the highest-ROI marketing channel for edtech — parents and teachers respond to email more than any other digital channel, and it is the most cost-effective way to maintain engagement with existing users while onboarding new ones.

The $1,000 would fund:
- **12 months of Mailchimp or ConvertKit** at the level needed to support 5,000+ subscribers
- **Automated email sequences**: Welcome series for new parents (3 emails), teacher onboarding sequence (4 emails), weekly learning digest template for active families
- **Parent acquisition campaign**: 3 targeted email campaigns to purchased lists of K-5 parent email addresses in Title I school zip codes (legally acquired through list brokers like DMA or ZoomInfo Education vertical)

**Expected outcome:** 500+ parent email subscribers; 10%+ open rate on automated sequences; measurable conversion to active accounts

### Use 3: Business Infrastructure — Accounting + Legal ($1,000)

As Learning Adventures moves from pre-revenue to revenue-generating, two infrastructure gaps need to be closed:

- **Accounting software** (QuickBooks Simple Start, 12 months): Proper invoicing for school partnerships, grant financial reporting, and revenue tracking — essential as the first school licenses come in
- **Business formation consultation** (1-hour with attorney): Ensure the business structure (LLC vs. C-corp) is optimized for the grant-funded growth phase and future investment conversations; review standard school licensing agreement template

**Expected outcome:** Clean accounting infrastructure for first revenue; business structure confirmed; school licensing agreement template ready to deploy

NASE members are not just running businesses. They are building community. The self-employed entrepreneurs in NASE's network are the coaches, tutors, consultants, and founders who make communities function — and who often serve communities that larger institutions overlook.
