# Age-Appropriate Content Guide

## Overview

Learning Adventures serves students from Kindergarten through 6th grade (ages 5-12). Content must be carefully designed to match the cognitive, emotional, and developmental stage of each age group.

**Key Principle**: Meet students where they are - not too easy (boring) or too hard (frustrating).

## Early Elementary (Grades K-2, Ages 5-8)

### Cognitive Development

**What they CAN do:**
- Follow 2-3 step directions
- Recognize patterns
- Sort and classify objects
- Count and perform basic addition/subtraction
- Recognize letters and simple words
- Understand cause and effect (simple)

**What they CANNOT do yet:**
- Think abstractly
- Hold multiple complex ideas at once
- Read lengthy text passages
- Understand sarcasm or irony
- Self-regulate frustration well

### Content Guidelines

#### Reading Level
- **Vocabulary**: Common, concrete words
- **Sentence length**: 5-10 words
- **Paragraph length**: 2-3 sentences maximum
- **Reading support**: Pair text with images; consider audio

**Good example:**
> "Click the red ball. Good job! Now click the blue ball."

**Bad example:**
> "Please select the spherical object that corresponds to the color red from the available options."

#### Visual Design
- Large, colorful graphics
- Simple, uncluttered layouts
- Big buttons and touch targets (60px+)
- Heavy use of icons and images
- Animated characters/mascots
- Minimal text on screen

#### Interactions
- Single clicks/taps
- Simple drag and drop
- Tap to select (not hold)
- Large target areas
- Immediate visual feedback
- No typing required (or minimal)

#### Feedback
- Instant, positive reinforcement
- Animated celebrations (stars, confetti)
- Audio praise ("Great job!")
- No punishment for wrong answers
- Gentle redirection ("Try again!")
- Lots of encouragement

#### Time and Pacing
- Activities: 2-5 minutes each
- Total session: 10-15 minutes
- Frequent breaks/transitions
- No time pressure
- Auto-save progress

### Subject-Specific Guidelines

**Math (K-2):**
- Counting objects (up to 100)
- Number recognition
- Basic addition/subtraction (within 20)
- Simple shapes
- Patterns (AB, ABC)
- Measurement comparisons (bigger, smaller)

**Science (K-2):**
- Animals and their features
- Plants and growing
- Weather (sunny, rainy, cloudy)
- Seasons
- Basic body parts
- Floating and sinking

**English (K-2):**
- Letter recognition
- Letter sounds (phonics)
- Sight words
- Rhyming
- Simple sentences
- Story sequencing (3 pictures)

## Upper Elementary (Grades 3-4, Ages 8-10)

### Cognitive Development

**What they CAN do:**
- Follow multi-step directions
- Read and comprehend paragraphs
- Perform mental math
- Understand basic abstract concepts
- Work independently for short periods
- Begin to self-correct errors

**What they're still developing:**
- Long-term planning
- Complex reasoning
- Extended focus (beyond 20 min)
- Emotional regulation with frustration

### Content Guidelines

#### Reading Level
- **Vocabulary**: Grade-level words; introduce some academic terms
- **Sentence length**: 10-15 words
- **Paragraph length**: 4-6 sentences
- **Reading support**: Some images; key terms highlighted

**Good example:**
> "Fractions show parts of a whole. When you cut a pizza into 4 equal slices and eat 1 slice, you've eaten 1/4 (one-fourth) of the pizza."

#### Visual Design
- Clear layouts with some complexity
- Balanced text and visuals
- Standard button sizes (44px+)
- Educational diagrams and charts
- Less animation, more information
- Color-coded organization

#### Interactions
- Multiple choice
- Drag and drop with precision
- Fill in the blank (numbers, short words)
- Simple typing
- Multi-step processes
- Some strategic decisions

#### Feedback
- Explanatory feedback ("Correct! 3 Ã— 4 = 12 because...")
- Progress tracking visible
- Stars/points/badges
- Gentle hints before showing answer
- Encouragement with guidance
- Celebrate effort, not just correctness

#### Time and Pacing
- Activities: 5-10 minutes each
- Total session: 15-25 minutes
- Some time challenges (optional)
- Clear stopping points
- Progress saves automatically

### Subject-Specific Guidelines

**Math (3-4):**
- Multiplication and division facts
- Fractions (introduction and comparison)
- Multi-digit addition/subtraction
- Basic geometry (area, perimeter)
- Word problems (1-2 steps)
- Data interpretation (bar graphs)

**Science (3-4):**
- Ecosystems and food chains
- States of matter
- Simple machines
- Solar system basics
- Life cycles
- Scientific method introduction

**English (3-4):**
- Reading comprehension
- Main idea and details
- Parts of speech
- Complete sentences
- Paragraph writing
- Beginning research skills

## Late Elementary (Grades 5-6, Ages 10-12)

### Cognitive Development

**What they CAN do:**
- Think abstractly
- Solve multi-step problems
- Read and analyze text
- Work independently longer
- Handle constructive criticism
- Begin metacognition (thinking about thinking)

**What they're still developing:**
- Complex abstract reasoning
- Long-term project management
- Handling ambiguity
- Advanced self-regulation

### Content Guidelines

#### Reading Level
- **Vocabulary**: Academic vocabulary; subject-specific terms
- **Sentence length**: 15-20 words
- **Paragraph length**: Full paragraphs
- **Reading support**: Definitions available; context clues

**Good example:**
> "The water cycle is a continuous process where water evaporates from bodies of water, condenses to form clouds, precipitates as rain or snow, and collects back into oceans, lakes, and rivers. This cycle is driven by the sun's energy."

#### Visual Design
- Information-dense layouts
- Data visualizations
- Minimal decorative elements
- Professional appearance
- Text-heavy is acceptable
- Tools and reference materials

#### Interactions
- Complex problem solving
- Extended typing/writing
- Multiple inputs per question
- Strategic decision making
- Experimentation and simulation
- Self-directed exploration

#### Feedback
- Detailed explanations
- Show work/reasoning
- Self-assessment opportunities
- Performance analytics
- Constructive correction
- Challenge progression

#### Time and Pacing
- Activities: 10-20 minutes each
- Total session: 20-30 minutes
- Time challenges more common
- Extended projects possible
- Student controls pace

### Subject-Specific Guidelines

**Math (5-6):**
- Fraction operations (all)
- Decimals and percentages
- Order of operations
- Pre-algebra concepts
- Ratios and proportions
- Coordinate graphing

**Science (5-6):**
- Earth systems (detailed)
- Human body systems
- Chemistry basics
- Physics concepts
- Scientific investigation
- Data analysis

**English (5-6):**
- Literary analysis
- Essay structure
- Research and citations
- Advanced grammar
- Persuasive writing
- Critical reading

## Difficulty Levels Explained

### Easy (Typically K-2)
- Minimal reading required
- Single-step problems
- Heavy scaffolding
- No time pressure
- Immediate, positive feedback
- Lots of visual support

### Medium (Typically 3-4)
- Moderate reading
- 2-3 step problems
- Some scaffolding available
- Optional time elements
- Explanatory feedback
- Balanced text/visuals

### Hard (Typically 5-6)
- Full reading comprehension
- Multi-step, complex problems
- Minimal scaffolding (hints available)
- Time challenges common
- Analytical feedback
- Text-heavy acceptable

## Language and Tone

### Vocabulary by Grade Band

| Concept | K-2 | 3-4 | 5-6 |
|---------|-----|-----|-----|
| Add | "Put together" | "Add" | "Calculate the sum" |
| Multiply | "Groups of" | "Times" or "Multiply" | "Find the product" |
| Subtract | "Take away" | "Subtract" | "Find the difference" |
| Equal | "The same as" | "Equal to" | "Equivalent" |
| Answer | "Answer" | "Answer" or "Solution" | "Solution" |

### Tone Guidelines

**K-2:**
- Warm, friendly, encouraging
- Use exclamation points
- Character/mascot voice
- "You did it!" "Great job!" "Let's try again!"

**3-4:**
- Supportive but more neutral
- Clear and direct
- Teacher voice
- "Correct! Here's why..." "Good thinking!" "Try one more time."

**5-6:**
- Respectful, informative
- Matter-of-fact
- Coach/mentor voice
- "Well done." "Consider this approach." "Review your work."

## Content Appropriateness

### Always Appropriate
- Positive themes (teamwork, curiosity, growth)
- Animals and nature
- Family-friendly humor
- Diverse representation
- Problem-solving scenarios
- Achievement and growth

### Use With Care
- Competition (avoid "losers")
- Time pressure (can cause anxiety)
- Fantasy violence (keep mild)
- Historical conflicts (age-appropriate framing)

### Never Include
- Scary or disturbing content
- Violence or harm
- Inappropriate language
- Stereotypes
- Religious or political content
- Commercial advertising

---

## Visual Content Guidelines

### Characters and People
- Diverse representation (race, gender, ability)
- Age-appropriate clothing
- Positive expressions
- Avoid stereotypes
- Include characters with disabilities naturally

### Themes and Settings
- Schools, homes, parks, nature
- Fantasy worlds (appropriate)
- Historical settings (accurately portrayed)
- Avoid: scary places, danger, conflict

### Animals
- Realistic or cartoon (not scary)
- Educational accuracy
- No harm depicted
- Diverse environments

---

## Assessment Guidelines by Age

### K-2 Assessment
- Observation of actions
- Completion (not accuracy alone)
- Simple correct/incorrect
- Visual progress (stars, stickers)
- No grades or percentages

### 3-4 Assessment
- Accuracy matters more
- Point systems
- Stars/badges
- Simple scores (8/10)
- Improvement tracking

### 5-6 Assessment
- Detailed scoring
- Percentage-based
- Performance analytics
- Mastery tracking
- Self-assessment opportunities

## Quick Reference: Grade Level Checklist

### Creating K-2 Content
- [ ] Text is minimal (2-3 sentences max per screen)
- [ ] Words are common and concrete
- [ ] Images support all concepts
- [ ] Buttons are large (60px+)
- [ ] No typing required
- [ ] Feedback is immediate and positive
- [ ] Session is under 15 minutes
- [ ] No time pressure

### Creating 3-4 Content
- [ ] Reading level is appropriate (Flesch-Kincaid 3-4)
- [ ] Instructions are clear
- [ ] Visual support for new concepts
- [ ] Standard button sizes (44px+)
- [ ] Limited typing
- [ ] Feedback explains reasoning
- [ ] Session is 15-25 minutes
- [ ] Time pressure is optional

### Creating 5-6 Content
- [ ] Academic vocabulary is defined
- [ ] Text can be longer
- [ ] Visual support for complex ideas
- [ ] Standard interfaces
- [ ] Typing is expected
- [ ] Feedback is detailed
- [ ] Session can be 20-30 minutes
- [ ] Time challenges are appropriate

---

## Red Flags: Content Review

**Stop and revise if:**
- Text is too long for grade level
- Vocabulary is too advanced
- Instructions require re-reading
- Interactions are too complex
- Feedback is discouraging
- Content could frighten young children
- Stereotypes are present
- Time pressure causes frustration
- Students consistently fail
