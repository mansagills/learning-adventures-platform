# AI Teacher Lesson — George Washington Carver Explains Crop Rotation

## Title
"The Secret Carver Kept in the Soil"

## Video Goal
Teach one focused concept: why farmers rotate crops (soil health, nutrient depletion, and how mixing in nitrogen-fixing plants like peanuts solves it) — through Carver's own discovery.

## Target Age
6-14

## Runtime
~2 min 45 sec

## Opening Hook
CARVER (warm, a little mischievous): "Can I tell you a secret the dirt told me? It changed how the whole South grew its food."

## Full Dialogue

**CARVER:** Can I tell you a secret the dirt told me? It changed how the whole South grew its food.

**CARVER:** Back in my time, almost every farmer grew the same thing, year after year, in the same field. Cotton, cotton, cotton. Made sense — cotton sold well. But nobody asked the dirt how it felt about that.

**CARVER:** *(kneels, scoops a handful of soil)* Soil isn't just... dirt. It's alive. It's full of nutrients, and plants eat those nutrients the way you eat breakfast. Grow cotton in the same spot every single year, and you're asking that soil to feed the same hungry plant, over and over, with nothing given back.

**CARVER:** Eventually the soil runs thin. Tired. And a tired field grows a tired crop.

**CARVER:** So here's what I found. Some plants — peanuts, soybeans, sweet potatoes — do something almost magical. They actually put nutrients *back* into the soil while they grow. Specifically, nitrogen, which cotton desperately needs.

**CARVER:** *(stands, gestures wide)* So instead of cotton every single year, what if a farmer grew cotton one year... and peanuts the next? The peanuts would rest and refill the soil, quietly, while still growing a useful crop. Then cotton could come back stronger the year after.

**CARVER:** That's crop rotation. Not a magic trick — just listening to what the ground actually needs.

**CARVER:** *(smiles, warmer)* I never imagined children more than a hundred years later would still be learning this in school. But here you are. And now — you know the secret too.

**CARVER:** Next time you see a garden, a farm, even a few pots on a windowsill — ask yourself: what does this soil need to keep going? That question alone makes you a scientist.

## Suggested Gestures
- Kneels and scoops soil during the "isn't just dirt" line
- Gestures wide when describing the rotation cycle
- Smiles warmly on the "children more than a hundred years later" line
- Soft, open-handed gesture on the final question, inviting the viewer to think

## Suggested Camera Angles
- Medium shot for the opening hook
- Close-up/push-in during the kneel-and-scoop soil beat
- Wide shot when Carver stands and gestures at the "cotton vs. peanuts" cycle
- Medium, straight-on for the closing direct-address question

## On-Screen Graphics
- Simple animated diagram: Year 1 (cotton field, soil nutrient bar draining) → Year 2 (peanut field, nutrient bar refilling) → Year 3 (cotton field, healthy nutrient bar)
- Icon set: cotton boll, peanut, soybean, sweet potato
- Keyword pop-up: "nitrogen" when first mentioned, with a simple one-line definition

## Sound Suggestions
- Warm, gentle acoustic/folk-instrumental underscore, upbeat but not busy
- Soft ambient farm sounds (light breeze, distant birds) under the opening
- A light "whoosh" or chime on the diagram transitions between years
