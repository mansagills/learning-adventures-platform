# Chapter 0 — "The First Spark"

*Status: initial draft, editable. Written with the `learning-adventures-narrative` skill as its first test case.*

## Scene 1 — Arrival
*[Hub: Town Square, day. A new student stands at the gates. Buildings visible in every direction — Math, Science, English, History, and the newer Interdisciplinary wing, all bright and busy.]*

**JAYLEN:** Hey! I'm Jaylen — I'm kind of the campus guide around here, unofficially. First day?
*[beat]*
**JAYLEN:** Nice. Okay, don't worry, everybody stands in that exact spot on their first day, looking exactly that lost. It's basically a tradition now.

## Scene 2 — Getting your bearings
**JAYLEN:** Couple quick things, then I'll let you loose. WASD or arrow keys get you moving — go ahead, try it, I'll wait.
*[player moves]*
**JAYLEN:** See? You've basically got it. Buildings are everywhere you look — walk up to a door, hit SPACE, you're in. Each one's got its own games, its own challenges.
**JAYLEN:** And that button up there — the little clipboard? That's your Quest Log. Anytime someone's got something for you to do, it shows up there.

## Scene 3 — Meet SPARK
*[A small glowing icon blips into existence near Jaylen's shoulder.]*

**SPARK:** [chirp] — hi. Question anytime. I'm around.

**JAYLEN:** That's SPARK. Kind of hard to explain SPARK exactly — think study buddy, question-answerer, occasional voice of reason. *[to SPARK]* Say something useful.

**SPARK:** [chirp] — XP for every quest. Coins too. Spend 'em at the shop. That useful enough?

**JAYLEN:** Look at that. Useful.

## Scene 4 — The Spark ignition
*[Player is free to walk toward any subject building. The moment they get close enough to one and something in it genuinely catches their interest — first game clicks, first "oh, I want to try that" — a small, warm light flickers awake somewhere near the player's chest. Barely visible. Easy to miss if you're not looking.]*

**JAYLEN:** *[stopping, actually surprised]* Oh — hey, whoa. You feel that?

*[player has no obvious reaction prompt — this is Jaylen noticing something the player might not even register yet]*

**JAYLEN:** That's called a Spark. Everybody's got one somewhere in them, but it doesn't usually wake up on day one like that. Mine didn't, not for months.

**JAYLEN:** It just means you got curious about something. Really curious. That's it — that's the whole secret. It's not a big ceremony, nobody hands you a cape. It just means you're paying attention.

## Scene 5 — The tell
*[Jaylen's tone shifts — lighter, but with a flicker of something underneath. This should read as small and easy to miss, not alarming.]*

**JAYLEN:** Can I tell you something? Probably nothing.
**JAYLEN:** Lately some stuff around here's been... quiet. Not broken-quiet. Just — a kid stops halfway through a question and can't remember what they were asking. A hallway feels grayer for an hour, and then nobody even remembers it happened.
**JAYLEN:** I've been keeping track for a while now. Might be nothing. But since your Spark's already up and running — you're actually one of the only people who could help me figure out if it's something.

**SPARK:** [low chirp] — logged. Filed under "probably nothing." *[beat]* Definitely something.

**JAYLEN:** *[laughs]* Yeah. Okay. Let's find out together.

---

## Briefing (shown before first quest — a short, low-stakes introductory Echo tied to whichever building the player's Spark ignited near)

*"Every Spark's worth following. Yours just woke up near here — so let's see what it's telling you. Nothing to be scared of. Just go see what's interesting."* — Jaylen

## Debrief (shown after quest clear)

**JAYLEN:** See? Told you. That wasn't so bad — that was just your Spark, doing exactly what it's supposed to do.
**JAYLEN:** *[quieter]* Keep an eye out for me though, yeah? If you notice anything... gray. You'll know it when you see it.

## Dev notes
- **Story item on first clear:** none yet — Chapter 0 is intentionally reward-light (XP/points only), since its job is onboarding and tone-setting, not plot-critical items. First story item should appear in Chapter 1 tied to Null.
- **Canon ties established here for future payoff:**
  - Jaylen's refrain **"Every Spark's worth following/noticing"** — reuse this line (or a twisted version of it) later, especially if a lieutenant tries to corrupt or mock it.
  - SPARK's dry "definitely something" — establishes SPARK's voice as clipped, a little deadpan, quietly more perceptive than it lets on.
  - The "gray" descriptor is planted here deliberately so Chapter 1's first real Hush encounter (Null) pays it off instead of introducing it cold.
