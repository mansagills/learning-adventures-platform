## Section 1: Business Overview

### What Learning Adventures Does

Learning Adventures is a free, gamified, COPPA-compliant educational platform for K–5 students. Students navigate a top-down 2D pixel-art school campus with a customizable avatar — entering subject buildings, playing curriculum-aligned adventure games, earning XP and achievements, and building the academic skills and STEM identity that shape their futures.

The platform currently includes:
- **41 curriculum-aligned educational games and interactive lessons** — free, verified, accessible on any device
- **A 2D campus world** (Phaser.js + Next.js 14) with a functional Math Building featuring 5 game stations
- **Parent dashboard** — real-time progress, goal-setting, weekly digests for working parents
- **COPPA-compliant child authentication** — anonymous usernames, PIN login, zero advertising, zero data collection on minors
- **AI content studio** (Google Gemini) — generates new standards-aligned games continuously
- **Social features** — leaderboards, study groups, friend challenges, daily quests

**Business model:** Freemium — permanently free core; premium subscriptions (~$5–8/month) and school licensing ($3–5/student/year) generate sustainable revenue.

### The Financial Inclusion Mission

Visa's foundation is built on the premise that financial tools and economic participation should be accessible to everyone — not just those who already have advantages. Learning Adventures is built on the parallel premise that educational tools should be accessible to everyone — not just the families who can afford premium subscriptions.

Both missions are about the same fundamental thing: the gap between what is available to advantaged communities and what is available to everyone else, and the conviction that closing that gap is worth building for.

Learning Adventures is a financial inclusion story as much as an education story. When a K–5 student from a low-income household builds positive STEM identity through Learning Adventures, the downstream outcome is access to STEM careers — the highest-paying, fastest-growing sector of the economy. Educational equity today is economic equity in 15 years. Visa's investment in Learning Adventures is an investment in that chain.

## Section 3: Economic Inclusion Impact

### Who Benefits

The Visa Foundation's equity mission centers on people who lack access to the economic participation that financial tools enable. Learning Adventures' equity mission centers on a population with deep overlap: children from households with limited financial resources, who are statistically less likely to develop the STEM skills and identity that open economic doors.

**Low-income families:** 41 free games. No subscription required. No credit card required. Every family gets the same experience as the family paying $15/month for a competitor platform — because there is no competitor platform that is genuinely free at this quality level.

**Women and mothers:** The parent dashboard was designed for the working mother. One view, one login, real-time visibility into her child's learning. The weekly digest email arrives in her inbox automatically. She does not need to be present, does not need to supervise, does not need to understand the platform deeply. She needs to know her child is learning — and the dashboard tells her.

**Minority communities:** Learning Adventures' culturally responsive content roadmap, bilingual interfaces, and community-centered marketing specifically target Black and Latino families — the communities where STEM engagement gaps are widest and the downstream economic consequences most severe.

### The Economic Multiplier

The connection between early STEM engagement and economic mobility is well-documented. STEM occupations pay, on average, 26% more than non-STEM occupations. Access to STEM careers is increasingly the difference between economic mobility and economic stagnation across generations.

When Learning Adventures builds positive STEM identity in a K–5 student from a low-income household, it is not just building an engaged learner. It is setting in motion a chain — stronger STEM performance → advanced STEM courses → STEM credentials → STEM careers → economic mobility — that Visa's equity mission is designed to support.

Every $1 invested in Learning Adventures creates measurable downstream economic opportunity for the underserved communities the Visa Foundation prioritizes.

## Section 4: Use of Visa Foundation Grant Funds

The $20,000 Visa Foundation / She's Next grant would fund four strategic initiatives:

### Initiative 1: Financial Literacy Content Expansion ($5,000)

Visa is the world's leader in digital payments and financial services. Financial literacy is among the most powerful economic tools a community can have — and one of the most underrepresented subjects in K–5 education.

Learning Adventures already includes *Money Market Madness* (money and counting skills) in the Math Building. The Visa Foundation grant would fund 5 new financial literacy games aligned to Visa's core educational themes:

- **Piggy Bank Planet** — saving, spending, and goal-setting for K-2 students; students manage a virtual piggy bank with simple income and expense decisions
- **Budget Battle Arena** — needs vs. wants, basic budgeting for grades 3–5; players allocate a weekly "budget" across competing priorities
- **Digital Dollars Quest** — introduction to digital payments, online safety, and responsible card use (grades 4–5); age-appropriate intro to how electronic payments work
- **Market Maker Madness** — supply and demand, price discovery, simple market simulation for grades 4–5
- **Credit Score Climb** — introductory concepts around borrowing, interest, and responsible credit (grades 4–5; highly scaffolded for age-appropriateness)

All games developed with AI studio + financial literacy curriculum specialist review; aligned to Jump$tart Coalition National Standards for K-12 Personal Finance Education.

**Expected outcome:** 5 new financial literacy games; Visa-aligned content in the platform; natural brand alignment for future partnership storytelling

### Initiative 2: Mobile-First Optimization for Low-Income Families ($6,000)

Pew Research data shows that 17% of Americans are "smartphone-only" internet users — concentrated among Black, Latino, and lower-income households. These are exactly the families Learning Adventures serves. A platform that isn't optimized for Android smartphones is not truly accessible to these families.

This initiative funds:
- **Progressive Web App (PWA)** development — enabling the platform to be installed on Android phones from the browser, app-like experience without the App Store
- **Android performance optimization** — full performance audit and fixes on mid-range Android devices (Samsung Galaxy A-series, Moto G-series — the actual devices in these households)
- **Offline game caching** — enabling students to download and play games without active internet connection; critical for families with intermittent data access

**Expected outcome:** Platform functions as an installable Android app; full offline capability; smartphone-only families unlocked as accessible users

### Initiative 3: Community Financial Institution (CFI) Partnership Program ($5,000)

Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs), credit unions, and community banks serving minority and low-income communities are natural distribution partners for Learning Adventures. These institutions are already trusted by the families Learning Adventures serves — and many have existing financial literacy education mandates.

This initiative funds:
- Outreach and partnership development with 10–15 CDFIs, community credit unions, and community banks in 3–5 states
- Co-branded "Financial Literacy Learning Adventures" program — positioning the financial literacy games (Initiative 1) as a resource CFI partners can offer to their customers' families
- Partnership materials (digital + print, English and Spanish)

**Expected outcome:** 10–15 CFI partnerships; distribution channel to underserved communities through trusted financial institutions; Visa-aligned co-branding opportunity

### Initiative 4: Multilingual Platform Expansion — Spanish + French ($4,000)

True economic inclusion requires linguistic inclusion. Learning Adventures' bilingual initiative extends the platform to Spanish-speaking families (the largest ELL group in U.S. elementary schools) and begins French-language development (for Haitian American, West African American, and French-Canadian communities).

- **Spanish interfaces for 15 additional games** (building on existing bilingual initiative)
- **French interface pilot for 5 high-priority games** — first French-language content on the platform
- **Multilingual parent dashboard** — key dashboard elements available in Spanish and French

**Expected outcome:** 20 bilingual game interfaces; French-language pilot launched; linguistic barriers reduced for two major underserved language communities
