## Section 1: Business Description

### What We Do

Learning Adventures is a free, gamified K–5 educational platform. Students explore a top-down 2D pixel-art school campus with a customizable avatar — entering Math, Science, English, History, and Interdisciplinary buildings to play through curriculum-aligned adventure games. They earn XP, unlock achievements, and level up characters, all while mastering the academic content their teachers need them to learn.

The platform includes 41 fully verified, curriculum-aligned educational games and interactive lessons — all completely free. It is built on a modern tech stack (Next.js 14, Phaser.js, PostgreSQL, Google Gemini AI), COPPA-compliant by design, and accessible on aging hardware and low-bandwidth connections. Parent and teacher dashboards provide real-time learning visibility. An AI-powered content studio generates new standards-aligned games continuously.

The business model is freemium: the core platform is permanently free; premium subscriptions (~$5–8/month) and school/district licensing ($3–5/student/year) generate revenue. We are currently in the pre-revenue scaling phase — the product is complete and operational; the current priority is outreach and user acquisition.

### Why It Exists

The edtech market has built its best products for the families who already have the most advantages. High-quality supplemental learning platforms cost $10–20/month — real money for low-income families. Free alternatives are either rote and disengaging or "free" in name only, funded by advertising targeting children.

Learning Adventures was founded to fix that: a free, safe, genuinely engaging platform built specifically for the students the market leaves behind — children from low-income households, communities of color, and under-resourced schools.

## Section 2: Underserved Business Status

Learning Adventures qualifies as an underserved business on multiple dimensions:

**Minority-owned:** The business is founded and operated by a person of color. Minority-owned businesses face documented disparities in access to business credit, venture capital, and institutional funding. According to the Federal Reserve's Report on Employer Firms, minority-owned businesses are approved for small business loans at roughly half the rate of white-owned businesses. Learning Adventures was built without access to traditional business lending or investor capital — built on skill, conviction, and sweat equity alone.

**Pre-revenue startup without bank loan access:** As a pre-revenue educational technology startup with no collateral or credit history as a business entity, Learning Adventures is categorically ineligible for traditional small business lending. The SBA reports that pre-revenue startups are denied bank loans at rates exceeding 80%. Grant funding is not a preference — it is a structural necessity for businesses at this stage.

**Mission serving low-income communities:** Learning Adventures' primary users are students from low-income households. The business model is intentionally designed to forego revenue from the families it serves most — keeping the core platform free — in exchange for the impact of reaching the students who most need it. This is a structural financial constraint that traditional investors are unlikely to fund.

## Section 3: Use of Grant Funds

The $2,500 Secretsos grant would be deployed across two immediate, high-leverage priorities:

### Priority 1: School Partnership Outreach Materials ($1,500)

School and district licensing is the primary revenue pathway for Learning Adventures. A single district partnership with 500 students generates $1,500–$2,500/year in recurring revenue. Converting the first partnerships requires professional outreach materials that the current budget does not support.

Specifically:
- **Professional pitch deck design** ($750): A polished, visually compelling school district pitch deck covering platform overview, curriculum alignment, safety compliance, implementation model, and pricing. Currently working from a basic template; a professionally designed deck significantly improves conversion rates with district administrators
- **One-sheet leave-behind** ($400): A single-page, print-ready summary of Learning Adventures for principals and curriculum coordinators — the document that stays on their desk after a conversation
- **Demo environment** ($350): Setup of a dedicated classroom demonstration environment on the platform allowing district administrators to experience the platform as a student would — the highest-conversion sales tool in edtech

**Expected outcome:** 3–5 school partnerships closed within 60 days of materials completion; first recurring revenue established

### Priority 2: Parent Acquisition Campaign ($1,000)

Before school partnerships, organic parent adoption creates the user base that makes the platform more compelling to school districts ("your parents are already using it" is the most powerful school sales pitch available).

- **Facebook/Instagram targeted campaign** ($700): 4-week campaign geo-targeted to parents of K-5 students in Title I school zip codes; creative assets designed around the platform's free access and safety features; objective: account signups
- **Community flyer distribution** ($300): Printed flyers for distribution at community centers, churches, libraries, and afterschool programs in 2–3 target cities; QR code linking directly to parent account creation

**Expected outcome:** 200–300 new parent accounts; measurable community presence in 2–3 target cities
