## Section 1: The Business and the Mission

### About Learning Adventures

Learning Adventures is the K–5 educational platform I built because the market wasn't building it for the students who needed it most.

Students explore a top-down 2D pixel-art school campus — navigating between subject buildings with a customizable avatar, playing curriculum-aligned adventure games, earning XP and achievements, and leveling up in a way that feels exactly like the games they already love. Because it is. It's a real game. It just happens to teach fractions and ecosystems and chemistry while it's at it.

The platform is free. Not "free trial." Not "free with ads targeting your child." Actually, completely, permanently free — because the families it is designed to serve cannot and should not have to pay $15/month for their child to access quality learning.

**What we've built:**
- **41 verified, curriculum-aligned educational games and interactive lessons** across Math, Science, English, History, and Interdisciplinary subjects
- **A fully operational 2D campus world** (built with Phaser.js) where students navigate as a customizable pixel-art character
- **Parent dashboard** — real-time progress tracking, goal-setting, calendar integration, and weekly digests — so working mothers stay connected to their child's learning without needing to be present every minute
- **COPPA-compliant child authentication** — anonymous usernames, PIN login, zero advertising, zero data collection on minors
- **AI-powered content studio** — Google Gemini–powered system generating new standards-aligned games continuously
- **Social features** — leaderboards, study groups, friend challenges, daily quests

The business model is freemium — the core platform is permanently free; premium subscriptions and school licensing generate sustainable revenue.

### The Mission

The students Learning Adventures is designed for are the ones the edtech market has consistently left behind:

**The daughter of a single mother** working two jobs, who can't supervise homework every evening. The parent dashboard tells her everything she needs to know in a single glance.

**The Black boy in a Title I school** whose Chromebook is from 2018 and whose school can't afford IXL. Learning Adventures runs on aging hardware, works offline, and is free.

**The girl who decided in third grade that math isn't for her.** The adventure-game framing meets her on her own motivational terrain — not demanding compliance with learning, but making learning genuinely worth choosing.

**The Latina child** whose mother speaks Spanish at home and can't help with English-language homework. The bilingual interface initiative puts support back in her mother's hands.

These are not edge cases. They are the majority of American K–5 students. And they deserve better than what the market has been offering them.

Her Agenda exists for women who are ambitious, who are doing meaningful work, and who are pushing through the specific barriers that women — particularly women of color — face in building businesses in America.

I designed a 2D game world. I built a multi-agent AI content studio. I architected COPPA compliance from the ground up. I wrote 41 educational games and lessons. I did it because the students needed it. And because I decided that "I don't have the funding" was not going to be the reason they didn't get it.

The Her Agenda Breakthrough Grant is named exactly right. I am at the breakthrough moment — the platform is complete, the product is ready, the mission is clear. What I need is the resources to break through from "built" to "reaching the families who need it."

## Section 3: How the $5,000 Would Be Used

Her Agenda's grant can fund marketing, legal, operations, or product development. Here is exactly how the $5,000 would be deployed:

### Use 1: Platform Mobile Optimization ($2,000)

The most underserved families access the internet primarily through Android smartphones, not computers. A Pew Research Center study found that 17% of Americans are "smartphone-only" internet users — concentrated among Black, Latino, and lower-income households.

Learning Adventures currently works on mobile browsers, but is not optimized for the mobile-first experience that smartphone-only families need. This funding would:
- Implement Progressive Web App (PWA) capability — enabling the platform to be "installed" on Android phones from the browser, with an app-like experience, no App Store required
- Optimize the parent dashboard for single-handed mobile navigation
- Test and resolve performance issues on mid-range and low-end Android devices (Samsung Galaxy A-series, Motorola Moto G-series — the actual phones in these households)

**Expected outcome:** Platform fully functional as an installed app on Android; smartphone-only families able to access the full platform experience; unlocks the largest untapped user segment

### Use 2: Her Agenda Community Activation ($1,500)

Her Agenda reaches ambitious women — many of whom are mothers, educators, or community leaders. This funding would activate that community as a distribution channel for Learning Adventures:

- **Her Agenda advertising** ($1,000): Sponsored content or newsletter feature in Her Agenda's channels reaching the exact demographic — ambitious women, mothers, community leaders — who are most likely to become Learning Adventures parent users and advocates
- **Community partnership outreach** ($500): Outreach to Black women professional organizations, Latina leadership networks, and women-of-color parent communities to introduce Learning Adventures as a free resource; materials in English and Spanish

**Expected outcome:** 500+ new parent accounts from Her Agenda community and partner networks; authentic earned advocacy from women leaders who experience the platform and share it

### Use 3: Legal Infrastructure — School Licensing Agreement ($1,000)

As Learning Adventures closes its first school partnerships, a professionally drafted school/district licensing agreement is essential. Currently operating from a basic template; a properly drafted, attorney-reviewed agreement:
- Protects Learning Adventures legally in district relationships
- Signals professionalism and organizational maturity to risk-averse district administrators
- Covers FERPA compliance, data handling, liability, and renewal terms — all critical for school procurement sign-off

**Expected outcome:** Attorney-reviewed school licensing agreement template; first 2–3 partnerships closed with professional documentation

### Use 4: Content Expansion — Girls in STEM Focus ($500)

Her Agenda is explicitly built for ambitious women. Learning Adventures' mission includes ensuring that girls from underserved communities build positive STEM identities early. This funding would develop 2 new adventure games specifically highlighting women in STEM:

- *Marie Curie's Element Laboratory* — chemistry game centered on the discovery of polonium and radium; players conduct experiments and solve elemental puzzles
- *Ada Lovelace's Algorithm Quest* — logic and sequencing game inspired by the first computer programmer; players write simple "algorithms" to solve narrative puzzles

**Expected outcome:** 2 new games featuring women STEM pioneers; girls who play see themselves as part of STEM history, not just its future

**Describe your business.**
