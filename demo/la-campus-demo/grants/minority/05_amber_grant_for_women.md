# Grant Application Narrative
## Amber Grant for Women (WomensNet)
**Amount:** $10,000/month + $25,000 annual grant
**Deadline:** Last day of each month (rolling — apply every month)
**Funder:** WomensNet
**Eligibility:** Women-owned businesses in the United States; any stage
**Apply at:** [ambergrantsforwomen.com](https://www.ambergrantsforwomen.com)

> **Program Notes:** The Amber Grant is one of the most accessible and well-known grants for women entrepreneurs. Each month, WomensNet awards at least $10,000 to a woman business owner. At year-end, all 12 monthly winners are considered for the $25,000 annual Amber Grant. The application is intentionally simple — it centers on **your story** and **your mission**, not on complex financials or academic research designs. Authentic, specific, personal storytelling wins here. The word limit is typically 500–600 words for the core narrative. This document provides a full-length narrative to draw from, plus a short-form version at the end ready to paste directly into the application form.
>
> **Eligibility note:** The applicant must be a woman who owns the business. Confirm this applies to the submitting founder/owner before applying.
>
> **Strategy:** Apply every single month. Monthly winners are chosen by WomensNet staff and a panel; persistence and fresh framing matter. Each month's application can emphasize a different aspect of the story — this document provides the full narrative; alternate short-form versions can be drawn from different sections each month.

### The Story

I built Learning Adventures because I was tired of watching the children in my community get left behind by a system that was supposed to serve them.

Not left behind by their teachers — who are working harder than ever. Not left behind by their parents — who love their children fiercely and would give them every advantage if they could. Left behind by an educational technology market that built premium products for families who already have advantages, and left scraps for everyone else.

I watched kids in under-resourced schools — bright, curious, capable kids — disengage from math and science in third, fourth, fifth grade. Not because the concepts were too hard. Because the tools available to them were boring, broken, or behind a paywall their families couldn't afford. I watched parents who worked two jobs try to monitor homework on a phone screen while dinner was on the stove. I watched teachers spend their own money on resources their district wouldn't fund.

And I decided: someone needs to build something better. It might as well be me.

### What I Built

Learning Adventures is a free, gamified K–5 educational platform where students explore a top-down 2D pixel-art school campus — entering subject buildings, playing curriculum-aligned adventure games, earning XP, and leveling up their character. It looks and feels like the games children already love. Under the hood, it teaches fractions, ecosystems, chemistry, and geometry — all aligned to the standards their teachers are responsible for.

The platform now includes:
- **41 fully verified educational games and interactive lessons** — completely free, every single one
- **A 2D campus world** (built with Phaser.js) — students navigate with a customizable avatar between Math, Science, English, History, and Interdisciplinary buildings
- **Parent dashboard** — real-time progress monitoring, goal-setting, weekly digests — so working parents stay connected without needing to be present every minute
- **COPPA-compliant child authentication** — anonymous usernames, PIN login, no advertising, no data collection on children
- **AI-powered content studio** — generates new standards-aligned games continuously so the library never stops growing
- **Social features** — leaderboards, study groups, friend challenges — because learning is better together

Everything is free. Not "free with a paywall after 10 minutes." Not "free with ads targeted at your child." Actually, genuinely, completely free.

### Who It's For

Learning Adventures is for the kid at the Title I school with the five-year-old Chromebook. The kid whose parent speaks Spanish at home and can't help with English-language homework. The kid who has decided — at age eight — that math "isn't for her." The kid who would spend hours in a game world if someone built one that actually taught her something.

I built it for those kids. I built it because I know what it means to be a capable child in an environment that doesn't have the tools to develop your capability. I built it because I believe that a child's zip code should not determine the quality of her learning experience.

### What I Need

The Amber Grant would fund the thing that every small business owned by a woman eventually needs most: the resources to get a working product in front of the people it was built for.

The platform is complete. The content is verified. The technology is solid. What I need is reach — the marketing, community partnerships, and outreach that puts Learning Adventures in front of the parents, teachers, and families who need it.

Specifically, the $10,000 would fund:
- **Community outreach** ($5,000): Targeted campaigns reaching parents at Title I schools; partnerships with community organizations serving underserved families; outreach to Black and Latina parent networks where the need is most acute
- **Spanish bilingual content** ($3,000): Bilingual interfaces for 10 high-priority games, serving the largest ELL population in U.S. elementary schools and ensuring Spanish-speaking mothers can support their children in their home language
- **Platform infrastructure** ($2,000): CDN upgrades ensuring fast loading on low-bandwidth connections — because many of the families I'm building for don't have fiber internet at home

### Why the Amber Grant

Amber Wigdahl died at 19, before she could realize her business dreams. WomensNet created this grant in her memory — to give other women the chance she didn't get.

I think about that when I think about the students Learning Adventures is designed to serve. Children who are bright and curious and capable, who — if we don't intervene — may reach adulthood without having had the educational experiences that would have opened different doors. The Amber Grant exists to give women a chance they might not otherwise get. Learning Adventures exists to give children a chance they might not otherwise get.

That alignment feels true and right. I would be honored to receive it.

## SHORT-FORM APPLICATION (500 words — paste directly into application form)

**Business name:** Learning Adventures
**What does your business do?**
Learning Adventures is a free, gamified K–5 educational platform where students explore a 2D pixel-art school campus, play curriculum-aligned adventure games in Math and Science, earn XP and achievements, and build the academic identity that shapes their trajectory for years. It's completely free — no paywalls, no advertising, no data harvesting on children. Just 41 games and lessons, a living game world, and a parent dashboard that keeps working moms in the loop even when they can't be present every minute.

**Tell us your story.**
I built Learning Adventures because I was tired of watching the children in my community get left behind by an edtech market that built its best products for families who already have advantages.

The kids I'm building for are in Title I schools. Their parents work two jobs. Their Chromebooks are five years old. The platforms that might actually help them are behind $15/month paywalls, or are "free" only in the sense of being funded by advertising targeted at children — which is not a tradeoff I'm willing to make.

So I built something different. I built a real game: a 2D campus world with a customizable avatar, XP and leveling, social features, and 41 fully verified educational games that teach fractions, ecosystems, and chemistry at the same level as platforms charging ten times what Learning Adventures costs (which is nothing). I built a parent dashboard so that the mom working until 8pm can see in one glance what her child did that day. I built COPPA compliance into the architecture so that parents from communities with justified privacy concerns can trust the platform with their children's attention.

I built all of it before anyone funded it — because it needed to exist and I decided not to wait.

**How would you use the grant?**
- $5,000: Community outreach campaign reaching parents at Title I schools and Black/Latina parent networks where the need is most acute
- $3,000: Spanish bilingual interfaces for 10 high-priority games — serving Spanish-speaking mothers who want to support their children in their home language
- $2,000: CDN infrastructure upgrades for reliable performance on low-bandwidth home connections

**Why does your business matter?**
By grade 5, children have already formed beliefs about whether math and science are "for them." Those beliefs shape their course-taking, their career choices, and their economic futures. The students most at risk of forming negative STEM identity are Black and Latino children in under-resourced schools — the exact students Learning Adventures was built for.

Every child who levels up in Learning Adventures is practicing being a STEM learner. That practice, repeated across hundreds of sessions in elementary school, is how STEM identity is built. And STEM identity is how economic opportunity is built.

Learning Adventures is a small business. It is also, in the most direct way I know how to say it, a bet on the futures of children who deserve someone to place that bet.
